8Usage Rules

The quick guide to keeping RealSight consistent.

  • Mint is the hero. Reserve it for the primary CTA, the FAB, active nav states, and the brand lockup. If everything is mint, nothing is.
  • Plan colours stay paired to plan tiers. Mint = Portfolio Pro. Blue = Adviser. Violet = Adviser Pro. Don't swap them in upsell copy or badges.
  • Green is functional, not decorative. Emerald (#22C55E) is only for positive YoY, growth, or confirmation — never as a background or decoration.
  • Destructive means destructive. Coral-rose (#FF5A7A) is only for delete, errors, or negative metrics. Don't use it for emphasis.
  • Gradients add variety, not noise. Each page picks ONE hero gradient variant — pages should feel layered, not loud. Keep body cards glass-neutral.
  • Surface hierarchy is sacred. Background → Card → Popover must always go from darkest to lightest. Don't mix up the stack.
  • Text tiers are capped at three. Foreground · muted-foreground · helper. Don't invent greys between them.
  • Amber = caution, not alert. Reserve amber (#F59E0B) for neutral-to-mixed signals and "proceed with care" states. It is not an error colour.
